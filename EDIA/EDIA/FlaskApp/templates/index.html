Early Diagnosis Of Autism




Autism is a neurological disorder that causes communication and socialize difficulties with others. Autistic children usually speak little, ignore others and come back again and again on the actions and behaviors such as spin in circles or focus on a particular object of hours. They excel in activities that require attention to the details, such as spelling or instruments but are upset when they are faced with a whole world of life

Early Signs


Passive rest

Lack of tears, lack of movement, lack of interest in the environment - often the baby looked comfortable,
showing signs of discomfort, hunger etc. Sleeping overnight during the first months of life

Overactive

Prolonged crying, physical restlessness, excessive movement. Sometimes babies seem passive during
their first year of life are becoming active in the second.
The study revealed that babies who cry a lot during the first year of life may be hyperactive in childhood

Reluctance or resistance to eat

A high percentage of children on the autism spectrum show different levels of difficulty eating - Refusal to breastfeed,
bottle or resistance of transition from liquid to solid food, eating from a limited menu.
In many cases, these difficulties can be seen already during early life

Accelerated growth of head circumference in relation to the starting point

In some cases, children diagnosed with autism are born with a relatively small head circumference,
but within two years may grow rapidly accelerated and reaches
large dimensions in relation to the head of children with normal development. This sign itself is not significant

Delayed motor development

Some babies with impaired motor development context whose development was fast, but these babies often
have hypotonia (low muscle tension), and delayed motor development.
This sign itself is not significant

Lack of direct eye contact with people

Baby with a developmental disorder engagement is not difficult to look at an object but may find difficult
to make direct eye contact with people. There are babies who need a longer
period of time (age 4-5 months) to mature visual channels

Reluctance to contact the parent or any other person

Some babies experiencing sensory flooding and therefore bother them physical contact.

Lack of response to the sound or the presence of a parent

There is no reference to the head, a smile or a murmur. Have to make a distinction between
the lack of response to the sound and the lack of response to their
presence - even if the baby does not hear it will react to their presence.
Anyway hearing test should be performed before drawing any conclusions
